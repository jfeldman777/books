# Age Barriers

An age barrier appears where the next level opens before the child, but the child cannot yet work freely on that level.

On the old level the child already feels cramped. On the new level he does not yet have enough stability, language, energy, self-control, and ways of acting.

Therefore the transition between ages is not only the accumulation of knowledge. It is a transition to a new type of tasks.

The table of levels and ages:

| Level | Structure | Age |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | One object | 0-3 |
| 2 | Many objects | 3-6 |
| 3 | One process | 6-9 |
| 4 | Many processes | 9-12 |
| 5 | One map | 12-15 |
| 6 | Many maps | 15-18 |
| 7 | One system | 18-21 |
| 8 | Many systems | 21+ |
