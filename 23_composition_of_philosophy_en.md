# The Composition of Philosophy

In my discourse, philosophy consists of four parts:

1. Metaphilosophy.
2. First philosophy.
3. Second philosophy.
4. History of ideas.

## Metaphilosophy

Metaphilosophy answers the question:

**what do philosophers think about philosophy?**

This includes definitions of philosophy, ideas about the tasks of philosophy, rules of philosophical discussion, and the distinction between discourse, excursus, and cosmos.

## First Philosophy

First philosophy considers the initial scene:

**the person - the world**.

Before society.

First philosophy has basic terms and terms that are introduced during unfolding.

Here we only indicate the basic terms:

- material - ideal;
- being and cognition;
- truth and truthfulness.

The unfolding of these tuples takes place in other texts.

## Second Philosophy

Second philosophy considers a more complex scene:

**the person - society - the world**.

Here a triangle appears.

Therefore, second philosophy is where themes appear that are impossible in first philosophy: progress, values, good and evil.

The basic terms of second philosophy are:

- [the arrow of history (four questions)](24_arrow_of_history_en.md);
- good and evil (three points of view).

Here too we only indicate the place of these themes. Their unfolding belongs to other texts.

## History of Ideas

The history of ideas is not only the history of philosophical ideas.

It includes the ideas on which I rely when building my philosophy: philosophical, mathematical, scientific, and cultural.

For example, Euclid and Hilbert matter not as the history of mathematics by itself, but as material for understanding how one can work with concepts.

## Short Formula

Metaphilosophy: what philosophy thinks about itself.

First philosophy: the person - the world.

Second philosophy: the person - society - the world.

History of ideas: the ideas on which the construction of philosophy relies.
