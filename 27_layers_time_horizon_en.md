# Layers of Being, Time Horizon, Strategies in Culture

In the pair **material - ideal**, we discuss ontology and epistemology.

In the pair **material - spiritual**, we discuss society and the person.

I call this **layers of being**. On closer inspection, there are not two of them, but more.

Here is the final list. Explanations follow below.

| No. | Layers of being |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Emotional |
| 1 | Material |
| 2 | Social |
| 3 | Cultural |
| 4 | Spiritual |

## Situations

What are these layers of being made of? They are made of situations.

Life is a continuous flow. A **situation** is a break in continuity.

If the situation is not forgotten and we continue to think about it, it becomes a **problem**.

Then a problem can become a **task**, if:

1. in this situation a person encounters things;
2. a clear criterion of success appears.

A problem can turn into a **meeting** if the one against you is a person.

A problem can turn into an **interview** if the one against you is a group.

Or if the one against you is a person, but you act not in your own name, but in the name of a group.

A problem can turn into a **match** if you act in the name of a group and the one against you is a group.

If you act in the name of a group, and the one against you is things, then the problem becomes an **agenda**.

## Time Horizon

Situations differ by the influence they have on our later life.

The simplest way to distinguish this influence is time.

We remember one situation for minutes, another for a month, a third for many years. I call this the **time horizon**.

| No. | Layers of being | Time horizon |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Emotional | minutes |
| 1 | Material | days |
| 2 | Social | years |
| 3 | Cultural | generations |
| 4 | Spiritual | millennia |

Here is a simple and clear example.

You published a book at your own expense. It has just been delivered from the printing house. Here it is, on the table.

The packages smell of printing ink. You close your eyes for a minute and enjoy the moment. This is the **emotional** layer.

The book has weight, price, paper quality. All this is **material**.

But then you carry the book to a bookstore, and, miraculously, they accept it. People begin to buy it. You are now famous. People recognize you in the subway. This is **social**.

But several years pass, and your book is forgotten. Your friend’s book is not. It is not only read, it is reprinted. And it is clear that it will be read for several generations. This is **cultural**.

Finally, we know books that have been read for thousands of years: the Old and New Testaments, the Quran, the Vedas. This is **spiritual**.

## Layers of Culture

Strangely enough, culture itself also becomes layered, and its layers can be compared with this table.

| No. | Layers of being | Time horizon | Layers of culture |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Emotional | minutes | Advertising |
| 1 | Material | days | Mass culture |
| 2 | Social | years | Tradition |
| 3 | Cultural | generations | Classics |
| 4 | Spiritual | millennia | Super-classics |

## Strategies in Culture

| No. | Layers of being | Time horizon | Layers of culture | Strategies in culture |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Emotional | minutes | advertising | Outsider |
| 1 | Material | days | Mass culture | Consumer |
| 2 | Social | years | Tradition | Transmitter |
| 3 | Cultural | generations | Classics | Author |
| 4 | Spiritual | millennia | Super-classics | Wanderer |
| 5 | Cosmic | millions of years | All | Invader |

The outsider exits the hierarchy. It no longer makes any demands of him.

The wanderer, by contrast, moves from bottom to top, then leaves this hierarchy and enters the next one from below.

The invader moves from any position to any other position in a short time.
