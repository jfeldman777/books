# One Key for Five Domains

The key that opens all doors, almost all of them, is the scale of **Levels**.

In education, this scale shows the optimal order for studying any subject:

begin with tasks of the first level, then move to tasks of the second level, and so on up to the eighth.

In psychology, we divide psychology into two parts:

- **general psychology** - how we all solve tasks;
- **differential psychology** - how we differ from one another when we solve tasks.

In general psychology, the model of the internal task conveyor works.

In differential psychology, three models work:

- Warming-Up;
- Encodings;
- Levels.

The scale of Levels shows not only differences between tasks, but also differences between people, ages, epochs, forms of education, strategies of behavior, and types of communities.

Therefore, it can serve as a common key for the five domains of the humanities:

- philosophy;
- psychology;
- education;
- history;
- everyday life.

This key does not erase the differences between the domains.

It makes it possible to see why they are connected and why the same structure can be found in different places.

The full unfolding of this topic begins with the model of Levels.
