# Static and Dynamic Strategies

Strategies in culture can be divided into static and dynamic strategies.

## Static Strategies

A static strategy works like this:

the author chooses a strategy, prepares for it, occupies a place, and holds on to it for life.

This is how the central positions work:

| Position | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Consumer | receives what is ready-made |
| Transmitter | passes it on |
| Author | creates something new |

## Dynamic Strategies

Dynamic strategies can be regular or irregular.

### Regular Dynamic Strategy: Wanderer

The wanderer moves upward through the hierarchy:

```text
consumer
transmitter
author
consumer in a new hierarchy
transmitter
author
...
```

He rises inside one hierarchy, then leaves it and enters a new hierarchy from below.

### Irregular Dynamic Strategy: Invader

The invader moves from any position to any position.

He does not have to follow the path from bottom to top.

## Outside Positions

The outsider is outside society, outside positions.

He exits the hierarchy, and it no longer makes its demands of him.
