# Recapitulation

The principle of recapitulation connects the history of humanity and the maturation of the child.

In its strong form, it does not claim that the child literally repeats the history of humanity. It claims that child development and the movement of history can be described by the same scale of levels.

If new levels of complexity are opened one after another in human history, then in the development of the child the next level opens as a possibility every three years.

Therefore age psychology, history, and education are connected not metaphorically, but through one and the same model of levels.
